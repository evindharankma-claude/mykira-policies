# Compound Interest Calculator

[← Back to MYkira](./)

Project the future value of savings or an investment when interest compounds over time, with optional regular contributions.

## What it computes

Given:
- **Initial principal** (your starting balance)
- - **Annual interest rate** (% per year)
  - - **Investment period** — in **days, weeks, months, or years**
    - - **Compounding frequency** — daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly, semi-annually, or annually
      - - **Optional regular contributions** at your chosen cadence
       
        - …MYkira computes the final balance, total contributions made, and profit (interest earned), and shows a year-by-year (or period-by-period) chart of growth.
       
        - ## Why short-term periods matter for Malaysians
       
        - Malaysian fixed deposits often have promotional rates for tenures shorter than a year. Tabung Haji and unit trust top-ups happen monthly. Bank Rakyat and short-term fixed deposit specials are typically 1–6 months. The Days/Weeks/Months/Years toggle on MYkira lets you model any of these directly, instead of forcing everything into whole years.
       
        - ## Example
       
        - **Scenario:** RM 10,000 initial principal, 6.0% annual interest, 10-year horizon, monthly compounding, RM 500 monthly contributions.
       
        - **MYkira output:**
        - - Final balance: ~**RM 100,560**
          - - Total contributed (principal + monthly): RM 70,000
            - - Profit (interest earned): ~**RM 30,560**
             
              - The exact figure varies depending on compounding frequency. MYkira applies the textbook fractional-period formula, so partial periods at the end of your investment horizon settle correctly.
